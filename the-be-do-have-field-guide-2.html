The BE → DO → HAVE Field Guide

A Practical Introduction to Identity-Based Transformation

About This Field Guide

Most people focus on outcomes.

  • More money
  • More confidence
  • More creativity
  • More success

This Field Guide introduces a different approach:

BE → DO → HAVE

A practical framework for creating change through identity first, action second, and outcomes third.

This is not a book to consume.

It is a guide to use.

Read slowly.

Complete the exercises.

Run the experiment.

Observe what changes.

What You’ll Learn

  • The BE → DO → HAVE framework
  • Why most people start with outcomes
  • How to choose an identity that can be observed
  • How to select one meaningful action
  • How repetition builds self-trust

Part 1: Understanding BE → DO → HAVE

The Framework

Most people begin with what they want to have.

  • Money
  • Confidence
  • Health
  • Creativity
  • Success

The BE → DO → HAVE framework reverses the process.

BE = Identity

Who are you becoming?

DO = Action

What does that person consistently do?

HAVE = Outcome

What results naturally follow?

Identity leads.

Action follows.

Results emerge.

Why Most People Start With HAVE

When outcomes become the primary focus, people often search for:

  • Better systems
  • New strategies
  • More motivation
  • Faster shortcuts

Yet outcomes are consequences.

They are not starting points.

A writer writes.

A creator creates.

A healthy person practices healthy behaviors.

The identity comes first.

Part 2: The Missing BE

Many people ask:

“What should I do?”

A more useful question is:

“Who would naturally create the result I want?”

Start with a problem or desire that feels real to you.

Then choose an identity that supports the outcome you want.

If I Keep Putting Things Off

Try:

I am someone who starts before I feel ready.

Examples:

  • Work on a task for five minutes
  • Send the email
  • Make the phone call

If I Never Finish What I Start

Try:

I am someone who completes things.

Examples:

  • Finish one unfinished task
  • Complete one small project
  • Cross one item off my list

If My House Always Feels Messy

Try:

I am someone who resets my space daily.

Examples:

  • Tidy for ten minutes
  • Put away ten items
  • Clear one surface

If I Want To Be Healthier

Try:

I am someone who moves my body every day.

Examples:

  • Walk for ten minutes
  • Stretch for five minutes
  • Take the stairs

If I’m Always Exhausted

Try:

I am someone who protects my energy.

Examples:

  • Go to bed on time
  • Take a proper lunch break
  • Reduce one unnecessary commitment

If I Want To Lose Weight

Try:

I am someone who makes healthy choices consistently.

Examples:

  • Eat one healthy meal
  • Drink more water
  • Add vegetables to lunch

If I Want To Write But Never Do

Try:

I am someone who writes regularly.

Examples:

  • Write for five minutes
  • Journal one page
  • Draft one paragraph

If I Have Ideas But Never Share Them

Try:

I am someone who publishes my work.

Examples:

  • Post one update
  • Share one insight
  • Publish one article

If I Want To Read More

Try:

I am someone who reads every day.

Examples:

  • Read two pages
  • Read for ten minutes
  • Carry a book with me

If I Want To Learn Something New

Try:

I am someone who practices regularly.

Examples:

  • Study for ten minutes
  • Practice a skill
  • Watch one lesson

If I Never Stick To My Plans

Try:

I am someone who keeps promises to myself.

Examples:

  • Complete one daily commitment
  • Follow through on one plan
  • Do what I said I would do

If I Want More Confidence

Try:

I am someone who does difficult things.

Examples:

  • Speak up once
  • Ask the question
  • Try something unfamiliar

If I Feel Scattered All The Time

Try:

I am someone who focuses on one thing at a time.

Examples:

  • Single-task for twenty minutes
  • Finish before switching tasks
  • Remove distractions

If I’m Always Overwhelmed

Try:

I am someone who handles one step at a time.

Examples:

  • Complete one next action
  • Break a task into smaller pieces
  • Focus only on today’s priority

If I Want To Be More Organized

Try:

I am someone who puts things where they belong.

Examples:

  • Put items away immediately
  • Organize one drawer
  • Spend five minutes resetting my space

If I Want To Stop Spending Unnecessarily

Try:

I am someone who spends intentionally.

Examples:

  • Track expenses
  • Wait 24 hours before buying
  • Review purchases

If I Want To Save Money

Try:

I am someone who pays myself first.

Examples:

  • Save a small amount
  • Transfer money to savings
  • Review my savings progress

If I Want To Grow My Business

Try:

I am someone who shows up for my business every day.

Examples:

  • Reach out to one person
  • Improve one offer
  • Spend fifteen focused minutes on growth

If I Want Stronger Relationships

Try:

I am someone who stays connected.

Examples:

  • Send one message
  • Make one phone call
  • Check in with someone

If I Want To Trust Myself Again

Try:

I am someone who follows through.

Examples:

  • Keep one promise
  • Complete one commitment
  • Do the thing I planned to do

Choosing Your Identity

Choose the statement that feels most relevant to your life right now.

Not the biggest problem.

Not the most impressive goal.

The one that would make the biggest difference if it improved.

Then commit to it for seven days.

  • One identity
  • One action
  • One week

Part 3: Building the DO Bridge

Once identity is chosen, action becomes simpler.

Choose one action.

Not five.

Not ten.

One.

Examples:

  • Write for ten minutes
  • Walk for fifteen minutes
  • Publish one piece of content
  • Read five pages

Your action should be:

  • Simple
  • Repeatable
  • Sustainable

Part 4: Understanding HAVE

Results often arrive later than expected.

The first changes are usually internal.

You may notice:

  • Greater consistency
  • Increased self-respect
  • Stronger self-trust
  • Less hesitation

External results often arrive afterward.

Measure progress by who you are becoming.

Not only by what you are receiving.

Reflection Questions

1. What Outcome Am I Chasing Right Now?

2. What Identity Would Naturally Create That Outcome?

3. What Action Can I Repeat Daily?

4. What Would Change If I Trusted Myself Completely?

Part 5: The Seven-Day Experiment

For the next seven days:

  1. Keep the same identity.
  2. Repeat the same action.
  3. Use the same time.
  4. Do not optimize.
  5. Do not redesign the plan.

Your job is repetition.

Not perfection.
