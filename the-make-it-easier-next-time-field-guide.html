Make It Easier Next Time

A Practical Guide to Improving Everyday Systems

Most people respond to recurring problems by trying harder.

They decide to:

  • be more disciplined
  • remember better
  • stay organized
  • pay more attention

Then the same problem happens again.

The keys go missing.

The laundry piles up.

The reusable bags are forgotten.

The project gets postponed.

The ingredients can’t be found.

At some point, it becomes worth asking:

Is the problem really me?

Or is the problem the system?

Who This Guide Is For

This guide is for anyone who finds themselves saying:

  • “Why does this keep happening?”
  • “I thought I solved this.”
  • “I keep forgetting.”
  • “This shouldn’t be this difficult.”

Especially:

  • neurodivergent people
  • caregivers
  • students
  • entrepreneurs
  • homemakers
  • busy humans

The Question

Whenever something frustrates you, ask:

What would make this easier next time?

Not:

How can I try harder?

Not:

How can I be more disciplined?

Not:

What’s wrong with me?

Just:

What would make this easier next time?

That question changes everything.

Example: The Missing Keys

Problem:

You spend five minutes looking for your keys.

Again.

Most people respond by saying:

I need to remember where I put them.

Try asking:

What would make this easier next time?

Possible answer:

Give the keys a home.

Now the problem disappears.

Example: The Forgotten Shopping Bags

Problem:

You reach the store.

The bags are at home.

Again.

Most people respond by saying:

I need to remember.

Try asking:

What would make this easier next time?

Possible answer:

Keep the bags in the car.

Or hang them on the door handle.

Now the system remembers for you.

Example: Never Reading

Problem:

You want to read more.

But you never seem to find the time.

Most people respond by saying:

I need more discipline.

Try asking:

What would make this easier next time?

Possible answer:

Leave the book where you usually sit.

Now reading requires less effort.

Example: Avoiding a Task

Problem:

You keep postponing something.

Try asking:

What would make this easier next time?

Possible answers:

  • Break it into smaller steps.
  • Prepare the materials beforehand.
  • Put it on tomorrow’s calendar.
  • Do the first five minutes now.

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is reducing friction.

Why This Works

Most people focus on changing themselves.

This question focuses on changing the environment.

Changing the setup.

Changing the process.

Changing the system.

And systems are often easier to improve than people.

The Seven-Day Experiment

For the next seven days:

Whenever something annoys you, pause.

Ask:

What would make this easier next time?

Implement one small improvement.

That’s it.

No spreadsheets.

No apps.

No productivity system.

Just one question.

One improvement.

One week.

See what happens.

Final Thought

Your life is full of recurring problems.

Some are unavoidable.

Many are not.

The next time something frustrates you, resist the urge to blame yourself.

Instead, ask:

What would make this easier next time?

Then make one small change.

Future You will thank you.
