Identity Shifts in Practical Steps

How to Become the Person Who Naturally Follows Through

Status: In Development

Format: Handbook

Estimated Length: 25–35 pages

Many people know what to do.

The problem is not information.

The problem is implementation.

This handbook explores a practical approach to closing the gap between knowing and doing.

Using the BE → DO → HAVE framework, readers will learn how to:

  • Choose identities that create real change

  • Build consistency without relying on motivation

  • Recover quickly after interruptions

  • Strengthen self-trust through action

  • Create lasting identity shifts through small daily practices

Who This Is For

This handbook is for people who:

  • consume more personal development than they implement

  • struggle to follow through consistently

  • start and stop frequently

  • want practical change instead of more inspiration

  • are tired of waiting to feel ready

What You'll Find Inside

  • The Knowing–Doing Gap

  • The Consumption Trap

  • The Observable Identity Rule

  • Identity Selection Method

  • One Identity, One Action, One Time

  • The Re-Entry Rule

  • The Seven-Day Identity Experiment

  • The Thirty-Day Identity Experiment

  • Reflection Worksheets and Implementation Exercises
