The Wild Womaniya Archetype Field Guide

Wild Womaniya Studio June 13, 2026

The Wild Womaniya Archetype

Discover the Feeling Your Website Should Create

Introduction

Many people start building a website by choosing colors.

Or fonts.

Or themes.

Or page layouts.

Most of the time, they’re starting in the wrong place.

Before you decide what your website should look like, you need to decide what your website should feel like.

A website is not just a collection of pages.

It is an experience.

And experiences are shaped by identity.

This guide will help you discover the feeling, atmosphere, and experience your website should create before you begin designing it.

Who This Is For

This guide is for:

  • creators
  • artists
  • writers
  • coaches
  • healers
  • freelancers
  • small business owners
  • anyone building a personal brand

Who This Is Not For

This guide is not for:

  • people looking for technical design tutorials
  • people searching for branding trends
  • people who want a ready-made template

How This Guide Will Help

By the end of this guide, you will:

  • understand the connection between identity and design
  • identify the feeling your website should create
  • make clearer design decisions
  • avoid copying other people’s brands
  • create a more authentic online presence

Core Observation

Many websites look attractive.

Far fewer feel aligned.

When people struggle with branding, the problem is often not design.

The problem is clarity.

Without clarity, every design decision becomes difficult.

With clarity, many design decisions become obvious.

Main Framework

Step 1: Identify Your Natural Energy

Ask yourself:

When people interact with my work, what do they consistently experience?

Examples:

  • calm
  • clarity
  • inspiration
  • courage
  • curiosity
  • comfort
  • creativity
  • depth

Choose three words.

Not ten.

Three.

Step 2: Define The Visitor Experience

Imagine someone lands on your website.

Complete this sentence:

“When they leave, I want them to feel…”

Examples:

  • encouraged
  • understood
  • energized
  • hopeful
  • reassured
  • inspired

Write down your answer.

Step 3: Look For Alignment

Compare:

What you naturally bring

with

What you want visitors to experience.

Look for overlap.

Example:

If your natural strengths are:

  • calm
  • clarity
  • simplicity

and you want visitors to feel:

  • reassured
  • supported
  • confident

your website should probably feel:

  • spacious
  • welcoming
  • easy to navigate

Not loud. Not chaotic.

Aligned.

Step 4: Translate Feelings Into Design

Examples:

If the desired feeling is:

Calm

Consider:

  • clean layouts
  • generous spacing
  • simple navigation

Creativity

Consider:

  • visual variety
  • expressive imagery
  • playful language

Trust

Consider:

  • clarity
  • consistency
  • straightforward communication

The feeling guides the design.

Not the other way around.

Step 5: Build Around The Experience

Every decision should support the experience.

Ask:

Does this:

  • image
  • headline
  • color
  • page
  • section

support the feeling I want people to have?

If not, remove or replace it.

Field Observations

Observation #1

Many people choose aesthetics before choosing experiences.

Observation #2

Copying someone else’s website rarely produces authentic results.

Observation #3

Clarity often creates stronger brands than originality.

Observation #4

People remember feelings more than design details.

Observation #5

Aligned websites are usually easier to maintain because decisions become simpler.

Practical Exercise

Complete these sentences:

My work helps people feel:

When visitors leave my website, I want them to feel:

Now compare both lists.

Circle any words that appear in both.

Those words are clues.

Quick Start Version

Today:

  1. Choose three words that describe your work.
  2. Choose three words that describe the experience you want visitors to have.
  3. Look for overlap.
  4. Make one website change that strengthens that feeling.

Keep it simple.

Checklist

Before changing your website, ask:

  • What feeling am I creating?
  • Is that feeling intentional?
  • Does this design choice support it?
  • Does this content support it?
  • Would removing something improve clarity?

If you’re unsure, simplify.

Next Step

Review your homepage.

Not your entire website.

Just your homepage.

Ask:

“What feeling does this page create?”

If the answer is unclear, start there.

Final Reflection

A strong website does not begin with colors.

It does not begin with fonts.

It does not begin with templates.

It begins with clarity.

When you know how you want people to feel, everything else becomes easier to build.
